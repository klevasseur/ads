Proof

Case I: If \(k = n\) we have \(P(n,n)=n!=\frac{n!}{(n-n)!}\text{.}\)
Case II: If \(0 \leq k < n\text{,}\)then we have \(k\) positions to fill using \(n\) elements and
  1. Position 1 can be filled by any one of \(n-0=n\) elements
  2. Position 2 can be filled by any one of \(n-1\) elements
  3. \(\displaystyle \cdots \)
  4. Position k can be filled by any one of \(n-(k-1)=n-k+1\) elements
Hence, by the rule of products,
\begin{equation*} P(n,k) = n \cdot(n - 1) \cdot (n - 2) \cdot \cdots \cdot (n - k + 1) = \frac{n!}{(n-k)!}\text{.} \end{equation*}
