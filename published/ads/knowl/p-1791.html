  1. The product \(A B\) is defined only if \(A\) is an \(m\times n\) matrix and \(B\) is an \(n\times p\) matrix; that is, the two “inner” numbers must be equal. Furthermore, the order of the product matrix \(A B\) is the “outer” numbers, in this case \(m\times p\text{.}\)
  2. It is wise to first determine the order of a product matrix. For example, if \(A\) is a \(3\times 2\) matrix and \(B\) is a \(2\times 2\) matrix, then \(A B\) is a \(3\times 2\) matrix of the form
    \begin{equation*} A B =\left( \begin{array}{cc} c_{11} & c_{12} \\ c_{21} & c_{22} \\ c_{31} & c_{32} \\ \end{array} \right) \end{equation*}
    Then to obtain, for example, \(c_{31}\text{,}\) we multiply corresponding entries in the third row of \(A\) times the first column of \(B\) and add the results.
