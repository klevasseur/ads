Exercise 7.

  1. If
    \begin{equation*} A =\left( \begin{array}{cc} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \\ \end{array} \right), X =\left( \begin{array}{c} x_1 \\ x_2 \\ \end{array} \right)\textrm{, and }B =\left( \begin{array}{c} 3 \\ 1 \\ \end{array} \right), \end{equation*}
    show that \(A X =B\) is a way of expressing the system \(\begin{array}{c}2x_1 + x_2 = 3\\ x_1 - x_2= 1\\ \end{array}\) using matrices.
  2. Express the following systems of equations using matrices:
    1. \(\displaystyle \begin{array}{c} 2 x_1- x_2= 4\\ x_1+ x_2= 0\\ \end{array}\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \begin{array}{c} x_1+ x_2+ 2 x_3= 1\\ x_1+ 2 x_2-x_3= -1\\ x_1+ 3 x_2+x_3= 5\\ \end{array}\)
    3. \(\displaystyle \begin{array}{c} x_1+x_2\quad\quad= 3\\ \quad \quad x_2\quad\quad= 5\\ x_1 \quad \quad+ 3x_3= 6\\ \end{array} \)
