We remind you of the relation on the integers that we call Congruence Modulo \(n\). If two integers, \(a\) and \(b\text{,}\) differ by a multiple of \(n\text{,}\) we say that they are congruent modulo \(n\text{,}\) denoted \(a \equiv b\pmod{n}\text{.}\) For example, \(13 \equiv 38\pmod{5}\) because \(13-38 = -25\text{,}\) which is a multiple of 5.
