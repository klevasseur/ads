Let \(G\) be cyclic with generator \(a\) and let \(H \leq G\text{.}\) If \(H = \{e\}\text{,}\) \(H\) has \(e\) as a generator. We may now assume that \(\lvert H\rvert \geq 2\) and \(a \neq e\text{.}\) Let \(m\) be the least positive integer such that \(m a\) belongs to \(H\text{.}\) This is the key step. It lets us get our hands on a generator of \(H\text{.}\) We will now show that \(c= m a\) generates \(H\text{.}\) Certainly, \(\langle c \rangle \subseteq H\text{,}\) but suppose that \(\langle c \rangle \neq H\text{.}\) Then there exists \(b \in H\) such that \(b \notin \langle c \rangle\text{.}\) Now, since \(b\) is in \(G\text{,}\) there exists \(n \in \mathbb{Z}\) such that \(b = n a\text{.}\) We now apply the division property and divide \(n\) by \(m\text{.}\) \(b = n a = (q m+r)a = (q m)a+r a\text{,}\) where \(0 \leq r < m\text{.}\) We note that \(r\) cannot be zero for otherwise we would have \(b = n a = q(m a) = q c \in \langle c \rangle\text{.}\) Therefore, \(r a = n a - (q m) a \in H\text{.}\) This contradicts our choice of \(m\) because \(0 < r < m\text{.}\)
