Theorem 15.1.15. Sun Tzuâ€™s Theorem.

Let \(n_1\text{,}\) \(n_2\text{,}\) \(\ldots\text{,}\) \(n_p\) be integers that have no common factor greater than one between any pair of them; i. e., they are relatively prime. Let \(n = n_1 n_2 \cdots n_p\text{.}\) Define
\begin{equation*} \theta:\mathbb{Z}_n\to \mathbb{Z}_{n_1}\times \mathbb{Z}_{n_2}\times \cdots \times \mathbb{Z}_{n_p} \end{equation*}
by
\begin{equation*} \theta(k) = \left(k_1, k_2, \ldots , k_p\right)= \left(k \% n_1,k \% n_2, \ldots, k \% n_p\right) \end{equation*}
where for \(1\leq i\leq p\text{,}\) \(0\leq k_i < n_i\) and \(k\equiv k_i\left(\textrm{mod } n_i\right)\text{.}\) Then \(\theta\) is an isomorphism from \(\mathbb{Z}_n\) into \(\mathbb{Z}_{n_1}\times \mathbb{Z}_{n_2}\times \cdots \times \mathbb{Z}_{n_p}\text{.}\)
