Answer 1.5.3.3.1.

  1. \(\frac{1}{1 (1+1)}+\frac{1}{2 (2+1)}+\frac{1}{3 (3+1)}+\cdots +\frac{1}{n(n+1)}=\frac{n}{n+1}\)

  2. \(\frac{1}{1(2)}+\frac{1}{2(3)}+\frac{1}{3(4)}=\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{6}+\frac{1}{12}=\frac{3}{4}=\frac{3}{3+1}\)

  3. \(1+2^3+3^3+\cdots +n^3=\left(\frac{1}{4}\right)n^2(n+1)^2\) \(\quad 1+8+27=36 = \left(\frac{1}{4}\right)(3)^2(3+1)^2\)
