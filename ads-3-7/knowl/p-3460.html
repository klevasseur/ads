If \(G'\) is connected, the induction hypothesis can be applied to it. If \(G'\) has \(v'\) vertices, \(r'\) edges and \(e'\) edges, then \(v'+r'-e'=2\) and in terms of the corresponding numbers for \(G\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{ll} v'=v & \textrm{ No vertices were removed to form } G' \\ r'=r-1 & \textrm{ One region of } G \textrm{ was merged with the infinite region when } e_1 \textrm{ was removed} \\ e' = k-1 & \textrm{ We assumed that } G \textrm{ had } k \textrm{ edges.} \\ \end{array} \end{equation*}
